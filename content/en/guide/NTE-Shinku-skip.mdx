<Section icon={Swords} title="Shinku Combat Mechanics & Playstyle Analysis">

To understand if Shinku fits your team, you must analyze her unique dual-form combat loop. Shinku does not generate ultimate energy like standard characters; instead, she operates on a specialized gauge system.

### The Dual-Form Loop: Normal vs. Surging Crimson
In her normal state, Shinku uses fast hand-to-hand martial arts to build up her **Defiant Spirit Gauge**. Once this gauge is full, she can activate her Ultimate, *Crimson Fury*, to consume the gauge and enter her legendary **Surging Crimson** (Rampage) state.

```
[Normal Form: Basic Attacks] ➔ [Build Defiant Spirit] ➔ [Cast Ultimate: Crimson Fury] ➔ [Enter Surging Crimson Form]
```

During her Surging Crimson state, her entire skill kit is replaced with enhanced, high-multiplier variants. She gains massive Area-of-Effect (AoE) capabilities, increased attack speed, and higher interruption resistance.

### The Menacing Gaze Mechanic
Outside of active attacking, Shinku accumulates **Deterrent Gaze** (or Menacing Gaze) stacks when she refrains from attacking for 2 seconds while in combat. She can hold up to 8 stacks, which are fully consumed upon her next strike to unleash an **Instant Strike**. This attack deals massive damage and instantly restores Ultimate energy, Skill energy, and Cycle Meter energy.

### Open World Utility
In the open world, Shinku can long-press her dodge button to activate her Rampage sprint, allowing her to run at incredibly high speeds across Hethereau without needing to summon a vehicle.

<Callout type="warning" title="Playstyle Warning">
Shinku's gaze stacks are completely lost if you swap her off-field before consuming them. Her early Awakening nodes resolve this clunkiness, allowing her to retain stacks off-field. Without this Awakening, her rotation can feel restrictive for players who enjoy frequent quick-swap team playstyles.
</Callout>
